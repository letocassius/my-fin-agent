# Earnings Reports: How to Read Quarterly and Annual Reports

## What Is an Earnings Report?

A quarterly earnings report (10-Q) or annual report (10-K) is a financial filing that public companies submit to the SEC. It provides a comprehensive overview of financial performance, condition, and operations.

Key reporting periods:
- **Q1:** January–March (reported ~May)
- **Q2:** April–June (reported ~August)
- **Q3:** July–September (reported ~November)
- **Q4/Full Year:** October–December (reported ~February)

---

## Key Financial Statements

### 1. Income Statement (Profit & Loss Statement)
Shows profitability over a period of time.

**Key line items (top to bottom):**
1. **Revenue / Net Sales** — Total income from products/services
2. **Cost of Revenue (COGS)** — Direct costs to produce goods/services
3. **Gross Profit** = Revenue - COGS
4. **Gross Margin** = Gross Profit / Revenue (%)
5. **Operating Expenses** — R&D, sales, marketing, G&A
6. **Operating Income (EBIT)** = Gross Profit - Operating Expenses
7. **Interest Expense** — Cost of debt
8. **Pre-tax Income**
9. **Income Tax**
10. **Net Income** — The "bottom line"

### 2. Balance Sheet
A snapshot of assets, liabilities, and equity at a single point in time.

**Assets = Liabilities + Shareholders' Equity**

**Current Assets (within 1 year):** Cash, accounts receivable, inventory
**Long-term Assets:** Property, equipment, intangibles, goodwill

**Current Liabilities:** Accounts payable, short-term debt
**Long-term Liabilities:** Long-term debt, deferred taxes

**Shareholders' Equity:** Common stock, retained earnings, additional paid-in capital

### 3. Cash Flow Statement
Tracks actual cash movement, divided into three sections:
- **Operating Cash Flow:** Cash from core business operations (most important)
- **Investing Cash Flow:** Capital expenditures, acquisitions, asset sales
- **Financing Cash Flow:** Debt issuance/repayment, share buybacks, dividends

**Free Cash Flow (FCF)** = Operating Cash Flow - Capital Expenditures
FCF is often considered the truest measure of financial health.

## Key Metrics to Watch

### Revenue Growth
Year-over-year (YoY) and quarter-over-quarter (QoQ) comparisons reveal growth trajectory.

### Earnings Per Share (EPS)
- **GAAP EPS:** Follows standard accounting rules
- **Non-GAAP / Adjusted EPS:** Excludes one-time items (stock comp, restructuring charges)

### Guidance
Forward-looking statements management provides about expected future performance. Markets often react more to guidance than actual results.

### Consensus Estimates vs. Actuals
Wall Street analysts publish consensus estimates for revenue and EPS. The "beat or miss" vs. these estimates drives immediate stock reactions.

- **Beat:** Actual results > consensus → typically bullish
- **Miss:** Actual results < consensus → typically bearish
- **In-line:** Actual ≈ consensus → muted reaction

---

## Earnings Call

Management hosts a live call to discuss results. Key elements:
1. **Prepared remarks:** CEO/CFO overview of results
2. **Q&A section:** Analysts ask questions

Watch for:
- Tone of management (confident vs. cautious)
- Commentary on macro headwinds
- Changes to full-year guidance
- Capital allocation decisions (buybacks, dividends, M&A)

## Common Earnings Metrics by Sector

| Sector | Key Metrics |
|--------|-------------|
| Technology | Revenue growth, gross margin, ARR, cloud revenue |
| Retail | Same-store sales, inventory turnover, gross margin |
| Banking | Net interest margin, loan growth, credit loss provisions |
| Real Estate (REITs) | Funds from Operations (FFO), occupancy rates |
| Energy | Production volumes, realized prices, EBITDA |

---

## Red Flags in Earnings Reports

- Revenue growing but margins declining
- Rising accounts receivable without revenue growth (channel stuffing risk)
- Negative free cash flow despite positive net income
- Frequent "one-time" charges that recur every quarter
- Management lowering guidance significantly
- Auditor changes or going concern qualifications
- Large goodwill impairments

---

## How to Read an Earnings Report Efficiently

1. Start with the **press release headline** (EPS and revenue vs. estimates)
2. Review **full-year guidance** (or next-quarter guidance)
3. Read the **income statement** for revenue, gross margin, and net income
4. Check **free cash flow** from the cash flow statement
5. Scan **management discussion and analysis (MD&A)** section for qualitative context
6. Listen to (or read the transcript of) the **earnings call**
