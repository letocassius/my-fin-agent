# Technical Analysis

## What Is Technical Analysis?

Technical analysis is the study of historical price and volume data to forecast future price movements. Unlike fundamental analysis (which focuses on financials), technical analysis focuses on price patterns and market psychology.

Key assumptions:
1. All known information is already reflected in the price
2. Prices move in trends
3. History tends to repeat itself

---

## Trend Analysis

A **trend** is the general direction in which a security's price is moving.

### Uptrend
- Characterized by higher highs and higher lows
- Each rally reaches a higher peak than the previous
- Each pullback stops at a higher level than the previous trough
- Signals: rising moving averages, positive momentum indicators

### Downtrend
- Characterized by lower highs and lower lows
- Each rally fails to reach the prior peak
- Each decline falls below the prior trough
- Signals: falling moving averages, negative momentum indicators

### Sideways / Consolidation
- Price moves within a horizontal range without clear directional bias
- Often occurs after sharp moves as the market "digests" the prior move
- Breakouts from sideways ranges can be significant

**Trend Duration:**
- Primary (major) trend: months to years
- Secondary (intermediate) trend: weeks to months
- Minor (short-term) trend: days to weeks

---

## Moving Averages

Moving averages smooth out price data to identify trends.

### Simple Moving Average (SMA)
The arithmetic mean of prices over a specified period.
- **20-day SMA:** Short-term trend
- **50-day SMA:** Medium-term trend
- **200-day SMA:** Long-term trend

### Exponential Moving Average (EMA)
Gives more weight to recent prices, making it more responsive to new information.

### Key Signals
- **Golden Cross:** 50-day SMA crosses above 200-day SMA → bullish signal
- **Death Cross:** 50-day SMA crosses below 200-day SMA → bearish signal
- Price above 200-day SMA: generally considered bullish
- Price below 200-day SMA: generally considered bearish

---

## Relative Strength Index (RSI)

RSI is a momentum oscillator that measures the speed and magnitude of price changes on a scale of 0 to 100.

**Formula:** RSI = 100 - (100 / (1 + RS)), where RS = Average Gain / Average Loss over 14 periods

**Interpretation:**
- RSI > 70: Overbought — potential for a pullback
- RSI < 30: Oversold — potential for a bounce
- RSI = 50: Neutral

RSI divergence (price makes new high but RSI does not) can signal weakening momentum.

---

## Support and Resistance

**Support:** A price level where buying interest is strong enough to prevent further decline. The market has "found a floor."

**Resistance:** A price level where selling pressure prevents further advance. The market has "hit a ceiling."

**Role Reversal:** When price breaks through resistance, that resistance level often becomes support (and vice versa).

**Key principles:**
- The more times a level is tested without being broken, the stronger it becomes
- Round numbers often act as psychological support/resistance
- High-volume price levels tend to be stronger

---

## Volume Analysis

Volume is the number of shares traded in a given period. It confirms price movements.

- **Rising price + rising volume:** Strong uptrend confirmation
- **Rising price + falling volume:** Trend may be weakening
- **Falling price + rising volume:** Strong distribution (bearish)
- **Falling price + falling volume:** Decline may be losing momentum

**On-Balance Volume (OBV):** Cumulative indicator that adds volume on up days and subtracts on down days. Divergence from price can signal trend reversals.

---

## MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

MACD measures the relationship between two EMAs.

**Components:**
- MACD Line = 12-day EMA - 26-day EMA
- Signal Line = 9-day EMA of MACD Line
- Histogram = MACD Line - Signal Line

**Signals:**
- MACD crosses above signal line → bullish
- MACD crosses below signal line → bearish
- MACD above zero line → uptrend momentum
- MACD below zero line → downtrend momentum

---

## Bollinger Bands

Bollinger Bands consist of a middle band (20-day SMA) and two outer bands set 2 standard deviations above and below.

- Price touching upper band: potentially overbought
- Price touching lower band: potentially oversold
- **Band squeeze:** Low volatility period, often precedes a major move
- **Band expansion:** High volatility, trend is accelerating
